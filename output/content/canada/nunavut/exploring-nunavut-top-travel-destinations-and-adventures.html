Destination Description Adventure
Iqaluit
Iqaluit, the capital of Nunavut, is a must-visit destination. It's a unique blend of traditional Inuit culture and modern amenities. The city is known for its Inuit art, history, and stunning landscapes.
 Dog sledding, snowmobiling, and viewing the Northern Lights.
Auyuittuq National Park
Auyuittuq National Park is a paradise for adventure seekers. It's home to Mount Thor, which has the world's tallest vertical drop. The park's rugged landscapes are perfect for hiking and camping.
 Hiking, camping, and mountaineering.
Baffin Island
Baffin Island is the largest island in Canada and the fifth largest in the world. It's known for its breathtaking fjords, mountains, and Inuit communities. Wildlife enthusiasts can spot polar bears, seals, and whales.
 Wildlife viewing, kayaking, and exploring Inuit communities.
Nunavut's Arctic Circle
Experience the magic of the Arctic Circle in Nunavut. The region offers a unique opportunity to witness the midnight sun in summer and the mesmerizing Northern Lights in winter.
 Viewing the Northern Lights, experiencing the midnight sun, and ice fishing.
Ellesmere Island
Ellesmere Island is the northernmost inhabited place in the world. It's a haven for wildlife, with a diverse range of species including muskoxen, Arctic wolves, and a variety of bird species.
 Wildlife viewing, bird watching, and exploring the Quttinirpaaq National Park.
