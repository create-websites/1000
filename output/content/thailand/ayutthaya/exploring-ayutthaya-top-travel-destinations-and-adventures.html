Ayutthaya Historical Park
Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Ayutthaya Historical Park is a must-visit. It's home to the ruins of temples, palaces, and Buddha statues, offering a glimpse into the city's rich history.
 Explore the park on a bicycle or take a guided tour to learn about the history and significance of the ruins.
Wat Yai Chai Mongkhon
This active temple is known for its large reclining Buddha statue and the towering chedi that can be seen from a distance. The temple grounds are peaceful and serene, making it a perfect spot for some quiet reflection.
 Join the locals in their morning prayers or climb the chedi for a panoramic view of Ayutthaya.
Wat Mahathat
Wat Mahathat is famous for the Buddha's head entwined within the roots of a tree. This iconic image is one of the most recognized from Ayutthaya.
 Take a leisurely stroll around the temple grounds and capture some memorable photographs.
Chao Sam Phraya National Museum
For history buffs, a visit to the Chao Sam Phraya National Museum is a must. It houses a vast collection of artifacts and treasures recovered from the ruins of Ayutthaya.
 Take a guided tour of the museum to learn about Ayutthaya's history and culture.
Ayutthaya Elephant Palace & Royal Kraal
Get up close and personal with these majestic creatures at the Ayutthaya Elephant Palace. Here, you can learn about the elephants and their importance in Thai culture.
 Enjoy an elephant ride or watch a traditional elephant show.
