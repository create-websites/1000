"Explore the Wonders: A Tour of the World's Most Majestic National Parks"

The article provides a comprehensive overview of 14 renowned national parks across the globe, highlighting their unique features and wildlife. From the world's first national park, Yellowstone, to the biodiversity hotspot of Manu National Park in Peru, each park offers a unique experience, be it witnessing the annual migration in Serengeti, exploring the world's largest coral reef system at the Great Barrier Reef, or hiking amidst the stunning glaciers of Glacier National Park.


National Park Location Description
Yellowstone National Park USA
Known as the first national park in the world, Yellowstone is famous for its geysers, hot springs, and diverse wildlife which includes grizzly bears, wolves, and herds of bison and elk.
Banff National Park Canada
Canada's oldest national park, Banff is known for its stunning turquoise glacier-fed lakes, snow-capped peaks, and abundant wildlife.
Galapagos National Park Ecuador
Home to unique species that can't be found anywhere else on earth, the Galapagos Islands are a living laboratory of evolution.
Kruger National Park South Africa
One of Africa's largest game reserves, Kruger National Park is home to a high density of wild animals including the Big 5: lions, leopards, rhinos, elephants and buffalos.
Fiordland National Park New Zealand
Known for its stunning fjords, waterfalls and wildlife, Fiordland is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers.
Great Barrier Reef Marine Park Australia
The largest coral reef system on the planet, the Great Barrier Reef is home to thousands of species of colourful fish, molluscs and starfish, plus turtles, dolphins and sharks.
Yosemite National Park USA
Famous for its waterfalls, deep valleys, grand meadows, ancient giant sequoias, and vast wilderness, Yosemite is a symbol of America's majestic natural beauty.
Serengeti National Park Tanzania
Renowned for its annual migration of over 1.5 million wildebeest and 250,000 zebra, the Serengeti offers arguably the most scintillating game-viewing in Africa.
Plitvice Lakes National Park Croatia
A forest reserve known for its chain of 16 terraced lakes, joined by waterfalls, that extend into a limestone canyon, Plitvice is a haven for diverse wildlife.
Torres del Paine National Park Chile
Known for its soaring mountains, bright blue icebergs that cleave from glaciers and golden pampas (grasslands) that shelter rare wildlife such as llama-like guanacos.
Komodo National Park Indonesia
Home to the world's largest lizard - the Komodo dragon, this national park is a diverse region with hiking trails, wildlife and stunning beaches.
Grand Canyon National Park USA
Known for its layered bands of red rock revealing millions of years of geological history, the Grand Canyon offers breathtaking vistas and hiking trails.
Manu National Park Peru
One of the most biodiverse areas in the world, Manu National Park is a haven for nature lovers with its unique variety of animal and plant species.
Glacier National Park USA
Known for its stunning glaciers, clear waters and wildlife including grizzly bears and moose, Glacier National Park is a hiker's paradise.
