"Explore West Bengal: From Cultural Hubs to Majestic Landscapes"

The article provides a comprehensive guide to five diverse destinations in West Bengal, India: Kolkata, known for its cultural heritage and colonial architecture; Darjeeling, famous for its tea gardens and Himalayan views; Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest and a UNESCO World Heritage site; Shantiniketan, renowned for its unique approach to education and art; and Digha, a popular beach town. Destination Description Adventure
Kolkata
Kolkata, the capital city, is known for its cultural heritage. It is home to numerous colonial buildings, museums, and temples. The iconic Howrah Bridge and Victoria Memorial are must-visit places.
 Boat ride on the Hooghly River, Tram ride, Street food tour
Darjeeling
Located in the foothills of the Himalayas, Darjeeling is famous for its tea gardens and the majestic view of the Kanchenjunga. The Darjeeling Himalayan Railway is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
 Trekking, Toy train ride, Tea tasting
Sundarbans
The Sundarbans is the world's largest mangrove forest and a UNESCO World Heritage site. It is home to the Royal Bengal Tiger and a variety of other wildlife species.
 Wildlife safari, Bird watching, Boat ride
Shantiniketan
Shantiniketan is a small town near Bolpur in the Birbhum district of West Bengal, and was once the home of Rabindranath Tagore. It is famous for its unique approach to education and art.
 Visit to Visva Bharati University, Cultural events, Local handicraft shopping
Digha
Digha is a popular beach town with shallow sand beaches and a beautiful coastline. It is a perfect place for a relaxing vacation.
 Beach activities, Seafood tasting, Visit to the Marine Aquarium and Research Centre
