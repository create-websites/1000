"Explore Gisborne: From Natural Slides to Wine Tasting Delights!"

The article provides a guide to six diverse attractions in and around Gisborne, New Zealand. It highlights the thrilling water sliding experience at Rere Rockslide, the serene nature walks at Eastwoodhill Arboretum, the local wine tasting at Gisborne Wine Centre, the cultural exploration at Tairawhiti Museum, and the breathtaking views from Kaiti Hill.


Destination Description Adventure
Rere Rockslide
Located just outside of Gisborne, the Rere Rockslide is a 60-meter long natural water slide. The smooth moss-covered rock, combined with the flowing water from the Rere Falls, creates a fun and unique sliding experience.
 Water sliding
Eastwoodhill Arboretum
The Eastwoodhill Arboretum is a tree lover's paradise. It's home to the largest collection of Northern Hemisphere trees in the Southern Hemisphere. The arboretum offers beautiful walking trails through the trees, perfect for a peaceful day out.
 Hiking and Nature Walks
Gisborne Wine Centre
For wine enthusiasts, the Gisborne Wine Centre is a must-visit. The centre offers a wide range of local wines for tasting and purchase. It's a great place to learn about the region's wine industry and discover your new favorite bottle.
 Wine Tasting
Tairawhiti Museum
The Tairawhiti Museum offers a fascinating insight into the region's history and culture. With a range of exhibits covering art, history, and Maori culture, there's something to interest everyone.
 Museum Visit
Kaiti Hill
Kaiti Hill, also known as Titirangi Reserve, offers stunning panoramic views of Gisborne and the surrounding area. A walk to the top is a must-do for any visitor to the city.
 Hiking and Sightseeing
