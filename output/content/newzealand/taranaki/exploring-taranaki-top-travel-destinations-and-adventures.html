"Explore Taranaki: From Majestic Peaks to Historic Paddocks"

The article provides a guide to five diverse tourist destinations in New Zealand, including the symmetrical Mount Taranaki for hiking, climbing, and skiing; the lush Pukekura Park for walking, picnicking, and boating; the scenic Surf Highway 45 for surfing, driving, and photography; the animal-rich Pouakai Zoo for wildlife viewing and photography; and the historical Governor's Paddock for a glimpse into New Zealand's colonial history.


Destination Description Adventure
Mount Taranaki
Mount Taranaki, also known as Mount Egmont, is a dormant stratovolcano in the Taranaki region. The 2518m high mountain is one of the most symmetrical volcanic cones in the world, giving it a similar appearance to Mount Fuji.
 Hiking, Climbing, Skiing
Pukekura Park
Pukekura Park is a Garden of National Significance, covering 52ha near the heart of New Plymouth. It's known for its diverse range of native and exotic plants. The park includes the popular Tea House on the Lake.
 Walking, Picnicking, Boating
Surf Highway 45
Surf Highway 45 is a scenic coastal route with numerous surf breaks. It stretches from New Plymouth to Hawera and offers stunning views of the Tasman Sea and Mount Taranaki.
 Surfing, Driving, Photography
Pouakai Zoo
Pouakai Zoo is a small zoo and animal park located in Kaitake. It's home to a variety of animals including monkeys, birds, and reptiles. The zoo is committed to providing a safe, happy and healthy environment for all its animals.
 Wildlife Viewing, Photography
Governor's Paddock
Governor's Paddock is a historical site where you can see the remains of the house of the first Governor of New Zealand, Sir George Grey. The site offers a glimpse into the early colonial history of New Zealand.
 Historical Tour, Photography
